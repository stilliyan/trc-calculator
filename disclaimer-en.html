Formulas and limitations

The calculator uses simple formulas for mg, mL, U-100 units, weekly averages, EOD averages of 3.5 injections weekly, blend breakdowns, schedule planning, and vial usage. These formulas describe mathematical conversion, not whether a dose is appropriate for any specific person.

U-100 syringes and measurements

The U-100 scale is treated as 100 units = 1 mL, meaning 1 unit = 0.01 mL. Different syringes or devices may have different markings, tolerances, or measurement accuracy. Always confirm the specific device before drawing any amount.

Blends and concentrations

In Blend mode, each compound is calculated from the concentration entered in mg/mL, while the syringe is based on total mL per injection. Inputs should match the actual label, composition, and concentration of the product.

Schedule, vial usage, and storage

The schedule and vial calculations are planning aids for educational use. They do not account for draw loss, dead space in a needle or syringe, measuring error, sterility, expiration dating, or storage requirements.
